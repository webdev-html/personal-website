links


links in html come in the form of an "a" tag, a stands for anchor tag, but most people just call it a link, because that is what it is.

when you make an "a" tag, the most important property is the "href" property, which tells the link what webpage it needs to take you to when you click it.

example:

click to go to google

by clicking that link, it sends you to google.

another important property of the "a" tags is a property called "target". what this property does, is that if the value of target is set to "blank_", it will make a new tab for the opened link, but if the target property is not used at all or blank, it will replace the website you clicked the link on to the linked website.

to name a link is very simple, after the "a" tag is made, go inbetween the two "a" tags(because the "a" tag is not a singular tag) then say what you want the link to say, like the link a few lines above says; click to go to google.
